# 08 — Success Criteria

The viewer must leave with six beliefs. Each is earned by a specific moment — no belief rests on assertion alone.

---

| # | Belief | Earned by | Test |
|---|--------|-----------|------|
| 1 | **The problem is real.** | Scene 1–2: an unattended agent moving £25,000 at 03:47; the same shape across healthcare, security, operations. | Viewer can name a system in their own org that acts irreversibly without review. |
| 2 | **Existing controls are insufficient.** | Scene 3: RBAC, monitoring, sampling, guardrails shown as reactive/probabilistic while Ω stays reachable. | Viewer accepts that "monitoring after settlement" is not prevention. |
| 3 | **Runtime Governance solves a different problem.** | Scene 4–5: reachability vs output; `R(t) ∩ Ω = ∅`; pre-execution interception. | Viewer can state the difference between "was the output bad?" and "can it reach a forbidden state?" |
| 4 | **The system is operational.** | Scene 5–7: live BLOCK and PERMIT, audit cards, exposure report, audit trail — not slideware. | Viewer saw a verdict and a regulator-ready record produced on screen. |
| 5 | **The claims are testable.** | Scene 8–9: 171/171 · 0 FP / 0 FN · patent GB2600765.8 · "Verify it yourself." | Viewer believes they can reproduce the benchmark, not just read it. |
| 6 | **The next step is a Runtime Safety Assessment.** | Scene 9: one concrete, low-friction action — a 48-hour assessment of reachable Ω. | Viewer knows exactly what to book and why now. |

---

### Single measure of success
A risk, security, or AI leader watches once and **forwards it internally with a request to book the assessment.** If the demo earns beliefs 1–5, belief 6 is the only rational conclusion: find out which catastrophic states are reachable in our system — before they become an incident.
